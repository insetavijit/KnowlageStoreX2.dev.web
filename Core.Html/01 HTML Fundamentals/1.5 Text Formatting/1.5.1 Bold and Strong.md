|---|---|---|---|
|**1.5.1 Bold and Strong**|Distinguish `<b>` vs `<strong>`|Semantic importance vs Presentational style|`<strong>` means important; `<b>` just means bold.|
|**[[1.5.1.1 Semantic Difference]]**|Meaning|Strong = Importance, Seriousness, Urgency|Use `<strong>` when the text is actually more important.|
|**[[1.5.1.2 The b Tag]]**|Presentation|Visual styling only (stylistic offset)|Use `<b>` for key words in a summary or product names.|
|**[[1.5.1.3 Accessibility]]**|Screen readers|Voice inflection|Screen readers may change tone for `<strong>`.|
|**[[1.5.1.4 SEO Value]]**|Ranking signal|Keyword emphasis|Search engines pay slightly more attention to text in `<strong>`.|
|**[[1.5.1.5 Visual Defaults]]**|Browser rendering|Both render as `font-weight: bold`|They look identical by default, but mean different things.|
|**[[1.5.1.6 CSS Alternative]]**|Best practice|`font-weight: bold`|If you just want it to look bold for design, use CSS.|
