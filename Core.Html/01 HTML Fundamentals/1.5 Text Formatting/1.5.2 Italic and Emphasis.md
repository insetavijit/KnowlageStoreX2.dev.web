|**1.5.2 Italic and Emphasis**|Distinguish `<i>` vs `<em>`|Semantic emphasis vs Alternative voice|`<em>` changes the meaning of the sentence; `<i>` changes the voice.|
|**[[1.5.2.1 Semantic Emphasis]]**|Stress emphasis|Changing spoken inflection|Use `<em>` for words you would stress when speaking ("I *love* HTML").|
|**[[1.5.2.2 The i Tag]]**|Alternative voice|Foreign terms, thoughts, technical names|Use `<i>` for taxonomic designations, technical terms, or thoughts.|
|**[[1.5.2.3 Accessibility]]**|Screen readers|Stress/Inflection|Screen readers may emphasize `<em>` content.|
|**[[1.5.2.4 Icon Libraries]]**|Common abuse|`<i class="fa fa-home"></i>`|The `<i>` tag is widely used (misused?) for font icons.|
|**[[1.5.2.5 Visual Defaults]]**|Browser rendering|Both render as `font-style: italic`|They look identical, but `<em>` has semantic weight.|
|**[[1.5.2.6 Nested Emphasis]]**|Degrees of stress|`<em>` inside `<strong>`|You can nest them to show strong emphasis.|
