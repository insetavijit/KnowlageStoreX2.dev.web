|**Subtopic**|**Focus & Purpose**|**Key Concepts / Details**|**One-Line Recall**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**1.4.8 Block vs Inline Elements**|Distinguish fundamental display behaviors|`display: block` vs `display: inline`|Block takes the whole width; Inline takes only what it needs.|
|**[[1.4.8.1 Block Level]]**|Attributes|New line, full width, respects margin/padding|Divs, Paragraphs, Headings are blocks; they stack like boxes.|
|**[[1.4.8.2 Inline Level]]**|Attributes|Flows with text, ignores vertical margins|Spans, Anchors, Strong are inline; they flow like words in a sentence.|
|**[[1.4.8.3 Inline-Block]]**|Hybrid|Flows like inline, sizes like block|Best of both worlds: sits on a line but accepts width/height.|
|**[[1.4.8.4 Nesting Rules]]**|Validity|Block inside Inline = Illegal (mostly)|You can't put a `<div>` inside a `<span>`.|
|**[[1.4.8.5 HTML5 Content Models]]**|Modern terminology|Flow content vs Phrasing content|HTML5 speaks in "categories" rather than just block/inline, but the concept remains.|
|**[[1.4.8.6 Visual Display]]**|CSS override|Changing defaults|You can turn a span into a block with CSS.|
